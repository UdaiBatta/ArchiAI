# Mumbai Residential Planning Notes (DCPR 2034)

## Setback Rules
Mumbai DCPR 2034 mandates front setback of 3.0m, rear setback of 2.0m, and side
setback of 1.5m each for residential plots under 500 sqm. Larger plots may have
proportionally higher setback requirements.

## Floor Area Ratio (FAR)
Maximum FAR for residential zones in Mumbai is typically 1.33 on R1-zoned plots.
Projects under cluster redevelopment or transit-oriented development may receive
higher incentive FAR, but this must be confirmed with MCGM approval.

## Parking Standards
One parking stall per residential unit is required under DCPR 2034 for buildings
up to G+2. Visitor parking at 15% of the total unit count is recommended but not
strictly enforced for plots under 500 sqm.

## Podium and Stilts
Stilt parking is commonly used in Mumbai to maximise ground coverage. The stilt
level is excluded from FSI computation if left open on at least 50% of its perimeter.

## Ventilation and Light
The NBC (National Building Code) requires every habitable room to have open window
area equal to at least one-eighth of the floor area. Mumbai's coastal climate
favours cross-ventilation from the west (sea face) when possible.
