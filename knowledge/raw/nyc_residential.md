# New York City Residential Planning Notes

## Zoning Resolution Setbacks
NYC zoning requires contextual front setbacks depending on the zoning district.
R6 through R10 zones use sky exposure plane and setback rules that progressively
restrict the building envelope above the street wall height.

## Floor Area Ratio (FAR)
Maximum FAR varies widely: R1-1 zones allow 0.5 FAR while R10 allows up to 10.0.
Inclusionary housing bonuses can increase FAR by 20-33% in Mandatory Inclusionary
Housing (MIH) designated areas.

## Parking Requirements
NYC recently eliminated minimum parking requirements for most new residential
developments, but existing lots in R1 through R5 zones may still require 0.5 to
1.0 spaces per dwelling unit. Always verify with the current Zoning Resolution.

## Fire Escape and Egress
NYC Building Code requires two means of egress for buildings over three stories.
Interior stairways must be enclosed with fire-rated walls in buildings taller
than 75 feet.

## Energy Code
NYC Local Law 97 mandates carbon emission limits for buildings over 25000 sqft.
Residential projects should plan for high-performance envelopes, efficient HVAC,
and electrification readiness.
